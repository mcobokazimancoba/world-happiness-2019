# 🌍 World Happiness Report 2019 - Exploratory Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project explores the World Happiness Report 2019 dataset to uncover patterns, relationships, and key factors influencing happiness across countries.

The analysis focuses on understanding how economic, social, and health-related variables contribute to overall happiness scores.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze global happiness distribution
- Identify top-performing countries
- Examine relationships between GDP, freedom, and happiness
- Detect patterns, trends, and potential outliers

---

## 📊 Dataset Description
- **Rows:** 156 countries
- **Columns:** 9 variables

### Key Variables:
- Happiness Score
- GDP per Capita
- Social Support
- Healthy Life Expectancy
- Freedom to Make Life Choices
- Generosity
- Perceptions of Corruption

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning
- No missing values were found
- No duplicate records detected
- Dataset remained unchanged after cleaning

✔ This indicates a **high-quality dataset**, suitable for reliable analysis.

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 🌟 Top Performing Countries
- **Finland** has the highest happiness score globally
- Other top countries include Denmark, Norway, and Iceland

### 💰 Economic Insight
- **Qatar** has the highest GDP per capita
- There is a **positive relationship between GDP and happiness**

👉 However, wealth alone does not fully determine happiness

---

## 📈 Statistical Insights

### 📊 Distribution of Happiness
- The data is **approximately normally distributed**
- Slight skewness suggests variation among countries

---

### 📉 Relationships Observed

#### GDP vs Happiness
- Strong positive correlation
- Countries with higher GDP tend to report higher happiness

#### Freedom vs Happiness
- Positive relationship observed
- Personal freedom contributes significantly to well-being

---

## 📊 Visualizations Included
- Bar chart (Top 10 happiest countries)
- Histogram (Happiness distribution)
- Box plot (Happiness spread)
- Scatter plots:
  - GDP vs Happiness
  - Freedom vs Happiness

---

## 🧠 Key Insight (Most Important)

Happiness is **multi-dimensional**:

- Economic strength (GDP) contributes significantly
- Freedom enhances life satisfaction
- Health and social support play critical roles

👉 No single factor explains happiness entirely

---

## ⚠️ Limitations
- No regional classification limits grouping analysis
- Dataset is observational (not causal)
- External factors (culture, politics) are not included

---

## 🚀 Conclusion
The analysis reveals that while wealth is important, true happiness is influenced by a combination of economic, social, and personal freedom factors.

Countries that balance these elements tend to rank higher in overall well-being.
