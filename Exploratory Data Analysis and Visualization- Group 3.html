🔷 PART G: INTERPRETATION¶

The dataset was cleaned by removing missing values and duplicate entries to ensure accuracy and reliability. After grouping the data by region, it was observed that certain regions consistently recorded higher average happiness scores, suggesting geographical or socio-economic influences.¶

The visualizations revealed clear patterns. A positive relationship exists between GDP per capita and happiness score, indicating that economic strength contributes to overall well-being. Additionally, freedom also shows a meaningful association with happiness, reinforcing the importance of personal autonomy in quality of life.¶

One key insight is that happiness is multi-dimensional; while wealth plays a significant role, factors such as health, freedom, and social support are equally influential in shaping global happiness levels.¶
